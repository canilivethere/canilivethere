Red flags

The hard truths, stated plainly — real risks, sitting right next to everything that's actually going well.

  • Judicial Reform Phase 2 (direct judicial elections)
    Yes
    Second round moved from 2027 to June 2028; Business Roundtable/US/Canada flagged USMCA-conflict concerns. criterion_id corrected 2026-07-15 (was missing entirely, a real extraction gap even though the underlying red-flags.md content already existed)
  • USMCA 2026 review outcome
    Trilateral joint review held July 1, 2026 — no consensus to automatically extend the agreement for a further 16 years; US opted for annual reviews instead, citing trade-deficit concerns
    CSIS, Holland & Knight, Steptoe, all 2026-07. Hard bargaining continues over automotive rules-of-origin, Chinese content routed through Mexican manufacturing, and existing steel/aluminum/car tariffs. Sheinbaum's stance is de-escalation, not confrontation — no retaliatory tariffs issued, has avoided the most severe blanket tariffs threatened, though under continued unresolved uncertainty
  • Peso currency stability, explicitly tied to USMCA review risk
    Traded in a narrow 17-18 MXN/USD band through 2026 so far, backed by over USD 257 billion in international reserves; risk framed as binary and directly tied to the USMCA outcome — an acrimonious breakdown could push the pair toward 20+
    IMF's own ARA adequacy metric rates reserves sufficient to absorb external shocks; ~300 basis-point interest-rate differential favoring peso carry trades; Banxico cut its overnight rate 25bps to 6.50% effective May 8, 2026. FXStreet and Disruption Banking both frame this as a textbook case of political/institutional risk with a directly-acknowledged binary currency-risk attached, unresolved as of one week after the July 1 review meeting
  • Political continuity — Sheinbaum administration
    Sheinbaum (Morena) won a strong 2024 mandate; no near-term election, coup, or leadership-crisis risk — next presidential election is 2030
    Real difference from a Milei-style case: no regime-change risk here, just live externally-driven trade/currency-policy uncertainty layered on top of genuinely stable domestic political continuity, plus the separately-tracked, slower-moving judicial-independence erosion (Judicial Reform Phase 2)
  • Foreign direct investment (FDI) resilience despite USMCA uncertainty
    23.6 $ billion, Q1 2026 record US FDI
    Foreign Policy, CSIS. EU separately committed to mobilizing ~$5.8 billion aligned with Sheinbaum's "Plan Mexico" strategy. A real counterweight worth naming plainly alongside the currency-risk finding above, not just the risk side — Sheinbaum has explicitly pointed to USMCA's continued (if unextended) validity as the source of investor "certainty"
  • Cartel/individual/police extortion, active
    Yes
    Trajectory qualifier per source: "worsening (not settling)". Police extortion itself now under official investigation — a genuinely different category of institutional-trust problem than cartel or freelance-criminal extortion. This is already the answer to "does risk specifically target foreigners here" — a confirmed Yes, not an unresearched gap, the sharpest such finding in Mexico's own research.     -76 % (vs. 2024)
    Genuine improvement, but real institutional friction (extortion, three separate categories) continues alongside it
